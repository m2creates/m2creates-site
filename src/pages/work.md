Every project here asks a version of the same question — and connects to the same throughline from a different angle. The analytical work draws on three schools of political economy: Hayek on the knowledge problem, Ostrom on institutional governance, Buchanan on constitutional constraints. Not decorative concepts. Diagnostic lenses.

## Analytical Portfolio

### ABC Inventory Segmentation Dashboard
*2026 · DataCo Smart Supply Chain Dataset · Google Sheets + Looker Studio*

ABC segmentation of 118 SKUs across a 180,000+ record supply chain dataset. Found that 6% of SKUs account for 77% of total spend — a sharper concentration than the classic 80/20 rule. Assigned differentiated replenishment and review policies by tier. Pareto curve, tier distribution donut, and policy recommendation table built in Looker Studio.

**The argument:** Treating unequal assets equally is not neutral — it is a policy choice with real costs. Ostrom's institutional analysis applied to the warehouse floor.

[Read the essay →](/abc-inventory-segmentation)

### Safety Stock & Reorder Point Calculator
*2026 · DataCo Smart Supply Chain Dataset · Google Sheets*

Configurable safety stock model using demand variability, lead time variance, and service level inputs. Z-score driven. Shows the buffer required to maintain fill rate targets when demand cannot be predicted with certainty.

**The argument:** Safety stock is an epistemological problem, not a math problem. Hayek's knowledge problem, buffered.

[Read the essay →](/safety-stock-epistemological-problem)

### Vendor Performance Scorecard
*2026 · In Progress*

Multi-vendor scorecard tracking on-time delivery, fill rate, and lead time variance. Composite scoring model with exception flagging for vendors crossing reliability thresholds.

**The argument:** A vendor scorecard is a constitutional rule system for supplier relationships. Buchanan on accountability made visible.

A decade of systems implementation for mission-driven organizations. Every engagement: replace what broke with something maintainable. Hand it off to non-technical operators. Leave documentation that outlasts the project.

*2019–2020 · Deytah · Washington, DC*

Full platform redesign for a DC-based policy and leadership organization. Four-dimensional filterable publications library, interactive global partner map, 40+ page sitemap, 13-template design system. Before: publications were unfindable and staff required developer help for routine updates. After: a fully self-maintained site with structured content governance. $16,500 contract. Site live at [hungercenter.org](https://hungercenter.org).
